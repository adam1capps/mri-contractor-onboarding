You're officially on the path to Roof MRI Certification. Done right, this training pays for itself immediately by delivering real value to your clients. Everything you need is on this page: how to prepare, how the day runs, what happens after, and the agreement that makes it official.

A1

Before training day

Four things to knock out this week.

Send your roof addresses

Training works best on a roof where you can deliver a real moisture report and create an immediate opportunity. Standard commercial flat or low-slope roofs only. No shingle, metal, or steep-slope. Ballasted and gravel BUR roofs are not great for training, but we can tackle one as a second option. You are responsible for securing roof access and making sure the roofs are safe for training.

Due 3 business days before training

Line up the training room

We meet at your office, or a nearby ABC Supply location if space is tight. Have a TV or monitor everyone can see, plus floor space to lay out a 4x8 sheet of insulation and membrane.

Equipment and PPE

Each trainee brings a qualifying moisture detection device. We'll send the specifications ahead of time; limited loaner devices may be available but are not guaranteed. On the roof: non-slip footwear for everyone, and fall protection wherever OSHA, local law, or your trainer requires it.

Confirm your crew

Tell us exactly who is training. Every participant signs a short field waiver before we step on a roof. You'll add your crew right on this page after the agreement below is signed, and each person gets their own signing link.

B2

How training day runs

One day, two halves: classroom in the morning, your roof in the afternoon.

8:00 AM

Meet and set up

We meet at your office or the ABC Supply location. Coffee helps.

8:30 to 10:30 AM

Classroom training

The fundamentals of Roof MRI: how the process fits into your workflow, why calibration is everything, and what makes this different from anything else in the industry.

10:30 AM

Hands-on indoor scanning

We start scanning the insulation and membrane laid out inside. This is where the method clicks and you see how Roof MRI behaves in real-world conditions.

12:00 PM

Lunch

We'll grab lunch together and talk shop.

Afternoon

Roof scanning on your building(s)

We head to one or two of your roofs and apply everything with live data. Walking a real grid, taking real readings, building your scanning rhythm.

D4

Scheduling and cancellation

There's real demand for these training dates. Here's how changes work.

Weather, with 48-hour notice

Free. We reschedule as soon as possible.

Cancellation inside 48 hours

Life happens, but others are waiting on your date. A $2,500 re-booking fee applies.

Day-of cancellation

If the trainer arrives and training can't happen for any reason, a new date is set and the full cost of the training is repaid.

This policy comes from experience. Clear communication keeps everything running smoothly.

E5

The Training Agreement

Read each section, then execute below. This is what protects both of us.

Plain-English summary: bring people who know roofing, keep your crew safe on the roof, keep our patent-pending methods confidential, certification is individual and stays active with two CTE credits per year, and the cancellation tiers above are binding terms of this agreement.

Client represents that each trainee participating in the Roof MRI training has a basic working knowledge of roofing systems and job site safety procedures.

Certification is issued to individual participants only and cannot be transferred to other persons or companies. A company may represent itself as having "Roof MRI certified staff" only if at least one of its current employees holds an active certification. If a certified employee leaves the company, the company may no longer claim to have certified staff unless others are certified.

(a) Continuing Education Requirement

Each certified individual must complete a minimum of two (2) continuing training education (CTE) credits per calendar year to maintain active certification status. CTE credit opportunities will be made available by ReDry. Failure to complete the required credits by December 31 of each calendar year will result in suspension of certification until credits are fulfilled.

(b) Company Association

Each certified individual's certification is associated with the company under which they were trained. The individual may only represent themselves as a Roof MRI Certified Technician while employed by or actively contracted with that company.

(c) Non-Transferability

Certification does not transfer to a new employer. If a certified individual leaves the company under which they were trained, their certification becomes inactive with respect to any new employer unless both of the following conditions are met: (i) the original certifying company provides explicit written permission authorizing the transfer, and (ii) the new employer is also a certified Roof MRI contractor in good standing.

No later than three (3) business days prior to field training, Client must provide a list of commercial flat or low-slope roofs for use in training. No shingle, metal, or steep-slope roofs will be accepted. Client is responsible for securing roof access and ensuring the roofs are safe for training. Failure to provide the roof list on time may result in cancellation or rescheduling of training.

Access to the full suite of scanning grids and MRI tools requires an active "Unlimited MRI Package" subscription. If Client opts not to maintain the subscription, Client acknowledges limited access and scanning ability. A 30-day complimentary trial is available for new trainees.

(a) Liability Waiver

All participants must sign a waiver acknowledging the risks of rooftop training, including potential injury or death. ReDry is not liable for accidents or injuries except in cases of gross negligence. Individual waivers are executed through this page before field training.

(b) Participant Fitness and Conduct

Participants confirm they are medically fit for rooftop activity. Trainers may exclude any individual who appears unfit or unsafe to participate.

(c) Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Participants must wear appropriate PPE, including non-slip footwear. Fall protection gear must be used where required by OSHA, local law, or trainer instruction. Access to the roof will be denied to anyone lacking required gear.

(d) On-Site Safety Procedures

Client must provide a safe roof access method (e.g. compliant ladder). ReDry may delay or cancel field activities due to unsafe conditions, including inclement weather.

(e) Age Requirement

Participants must be 18 years of age or older.

All training materials, methods, and the Roof MRI process are proprietary and patent-pending. No reproduction, external teaching, sublicensing, or redistribution is allowed. Materials must be handled as confidential and may not be recorded or shared without ReDry's written permission. Unauthorized use may result in legal action and revocation of certification.

Each trainee must bring a qualifying moisture detection device. ReDry will provide equipment specifications beforehand. Limited loaner devices may be available but are not guaranteed. Any borrowed equipment must be returned in original condition or will be billed to Client.

Reschedules due to weather with at least 48 hours' notice are free of charge and will be rescheduled as soon as possible. Cancellations inside of 48 hours will incur a $2,500 re-booking fee. If the trainer arrives on the scheduled date and training cannot proceed for any reason attributable to Client, a new date must be set and Client must repay the full cost of the training.

(a) Governing Law

This Agreement will be governed by the laws of the state in which ReDry is headquartered, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

(b) Entire Agreement

This document represents the full understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements regarding training.

(c) Amendments

This Agreement may be modified only in writing signed by both parties.
